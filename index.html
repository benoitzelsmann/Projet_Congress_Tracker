Why Quant Analysts Choose Capitol Quant

Official government sources like the Senate Electronic Financial Disclosures (EFD) and House databases are intentionally fragmented. Data is often locked in non-searchable, handwritten PDFs, creating a massive barrier for retail investors and algorithmic traders.

Our ETL pipeline bypasses these limitations by performing advanced OCR (Optical Character Recognition) and Ticker Normalization. We map ambiguous asset names to standardized Ticker Symbols and calculate the "Reporting Lag"—the delta between the trade execution and its public filing. This allows you to identify which members of Congress are consistently outperforming the market before the public even knows a trade occurred.

Relational Architecture

Why the 'politicians' folder?

Instead of repeating "Nancy Pelosi" 1,000 times in the transaction log, we use a normalized structure:

  • 1. The "Phonebook" (politicians.csv) Contains unique IDs, Total Trade Volume, and Transaction Counts for every legislator.
  • 2. Reporting Lag Calculation We calculate the exact delay (in days) between the Trade Date and Publication Date.
This metric identifies members who consistently delay disclosures vs. those who report instantly.
